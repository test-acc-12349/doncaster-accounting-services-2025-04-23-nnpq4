Understanding Business Valuation: A Comprehensive Guide

Published on April 23, 2025

Business valuation is a crucial process for determining a company's economic worth, essential for sales, investments, and strategic planning. Three primary methods are used: the asset-based approach, which totals tangible and intangible assets minus liabilities; the market approach, comparing similar company sales; and the income approach, focusing on future earning potential. Key factors affecting valuation include financial performance, market conditions, business model, management team, intellectual property, and customer base. Professional valuators bring expertise and credibility to the process, particularly important for legal compliance, tax purposes, and stakeholder confidence in major business decisions.
